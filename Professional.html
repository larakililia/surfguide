The maneuvers that you should practice for the Professional level:



Aerial:


To learn how to do an aerial in surf, you need a short board, a light on-shore breeze over the lip of the selected wave, and speed. The winning formula for aerial maneuver is as follow:
  • Take off in a steep two-to-three foot wave;
  • Pump for speed and target a closing section or a breaking wave lip;
  • Maintain a straight oblique surf line towards the selected lunch pad;
  • Widen your stance, kick the tail with your feet and about 66% of your weight, let the surfboard leave the lip and raise your knees so that your feets are at the same level as your chest;
  • Control the excessive weight of your back foot with a slight push of the front foot over the board;
  • As your surfboard takes to the air, smoothly drive it to the landing site;
  • Grab a rail to correct the trajectory, if needed;
  • Turn your eyes, head and body to the flats;
  • Stay low, and bend your knees and torso to prepare for landing impact;
  • Distribute your weight over both feet and finish the move.



Reverse:


The reverse 360 allows you to spin down a wave, and they can be executed at the beginning or the end of a ride. Here's how to do a frontside reverse 360:
  • Take off on a good-looking waist-high wave;
  • Bottom turn, get a fair amount of speed, and aim to the lip at 45 degrees;
  • Start rotating your upper body towards the base of the wave;
  • As you reach the lip of the wave, transfer your weight to the front feet to free and release pressure over the fins;
  • Keep rotating the body until the tail of the board starts spinning;
  • As you feel the board turning, bend your front knee and get your leading hand in the water to help accelerate the 360;
  • Start transfer your weight to the back foot, and let the fins catch back in the water and work as a pivoting axis;
  • Adopt a low stance, let your front foot complete the 180-degree turn, and resume riding;
    • Remember to transfer the weight of your body down to the bottom of the wave while performing the first half of the turn. If you struggle to initiate or complete the rotation, grab your rail to keep the board under control and let your body do the rest.



    Backflip:


    To do a backflip, you have to go forward with fastness on the wave’s lip. If you execute it correctly, you are going to be in the air for a moment, you have to enjoy this moment to make a rotation of your body. You can catch the board to help you. And don’t forget to bed your knees. You must have made an entire turn on yourself before the land.
